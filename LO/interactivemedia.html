PORTFOLIO

You can find the feedback and figma files in the sources section below.

For my portfolio, I asked for some feedback about the site. I also conducted a user test with a few people. I did this with an interactive prototype in Figma. A user test was conducted with Dirk Norg, which is in my Feedpulse. I also used my site as a prototype. I received feedback regarding the navbar and how it navigates to parts of the page. The navbar was not very neat and was a bit too much, so I adjusted the opacity and added a border.

Furthermore, the site did not scroll well to the parts of the page; I fixed this with padding so that the navbar does not overlap with the title. After applying all the feedback, everything was legible and clear. The navbar now also better fits the site and is less distracting.

PROJECT
BRANDING

Here I experimented a lot with logos and showed them to the stakeholder and students together with Loes. We used a CMD method, a survey. With that survey, they could choose between the logos and give feedback. Based on that feedback, two logos were favored. With this feedback and logos, I could create a new logo. My logo did not become the final logo. However, I did make the poster for which we received feedback from the stakeholder. I applied this feedback and made a new one, which became the final poster.

Thanks to the CMD method prototyping, we discovered that the logo was not easily readable on a shirt or sweater. If you put the logo on a shirt or sweater, for example, the text would be hard to read and the soundwave would become pixelated. We also asked the stakeholder for feedback, and they agreed with us. The solution was to only use the text on a shirt or sweater, so the name would still be there without the soundwave interfering.

I also created a persona for the soundlab project. I did this with the CMD method persona. This way I could see what the target group wanted for the branding and what they would like. With a persona in place, I can tailor the messaging and communication strategies to resonate with the target audience. Understanding their preferences and communication style allows me to craft content that are more engaging and relevant to the target audience.

PROJECT
MEDIA CAMPAIGN

Here I looked at which media platform the part-time students used the most; I did this with the interview Job conducted. By using the interview CMD method, you learn where best to advertise for the program. I also looked at what information the students were missing but wanted to know before starting the program.

With this, I could create a LinkedIn post that would attract them. This had to be in the Fontys house style, which I found on the site. As a design platform, I used Adobe Illustrator. We showed all the designs of the posts to the stakeholder, who gave us feedback that it looked neat and wants to try the method as well.

PROJECT-X

For project-X i'm making a stardew valley mod, I needed to understand color theory and design principles to ensure the mod seamlessly integrates into the game's environment. I also researched the modding tools for the game. This would be SMAPI. I also need to understand the game's mechanics and how the mod would interact with them. And of course test the mod on players to get feedback. For pixel art i need to focus on color palettes and shading.

CMD METHODS

For CMD methods, I used survey, prototyping and persona.

For the survey, Loes and I created a survey for the branding. In it, the students and stakeholders could choose between the logos and give feedback. Based on that feedback, we created a new logo. I chose the survey because I wanted to get a lot of feedback and answers in a short time.
For prototyping, I used my portfolio as a prototype. I could ask for feedback about the site and the navbar. I did this with an interactive prototype in Figma.
